Wellbeing & Personal Development

Graduate school is more than just academics. It's important to cultivate a balanced life. Balance your academic life with engaging extracurricular activities, community involvement, and personal development opportunities. Below are some resources to help you enjoy life outside the classroom:


Physical Health

Keeping your body healthy is essential for success. The University of Michigan offers numerous resources to help you stay in top physical condition:

University Health Service (UHS)

University Health Service (UHS): Access comprehensive medical care, including routine check-ups, vaccinations, and specialist referrals.

Fitness and Recreational Facilities

Participate in various fitness programs, sports clubs, and use of gym facilities to stay active and healthy. University of Michigan Recreational Facilities:

  • Palmer Field
  • Mitchell Field
  • Baits Tennis Courts
  • Intramural Sports Building
  • North Campus Recreational Building
  • Sports Coliseum
  • Canham Natatorium

Mental Health

Maintaining your mental well-being is crucial for thriving in graduate school. Here are resources to support your mental health:

CAPS (Counseling and Psychological Services)

University Health & Counseling (UHC) supports the health and well-being of the campus community. CAPS is inspired to help students realize their potential, cope with the stresses of life, work productively, and connect meaningfully with others. Access individual counseling, group therapy, workshops, and crisis intervention services.

Phone: 734-764-8312 (24hrs)
Email: caps-uofm@umich.edu

Address: Michigan Union
530 South State Street, Suite 4079
Ann Arbor, MI 48109

CEW+ (Center for the Education of Women)

CEW+ empowers women+ and underserved individuals in the University of Michigan and surrounding communities by serving as an advocate and providing resources to help individuals reach their academic, financial, and professional potential.

Phone: 734-764-6360
Email: cew.appts@umich.edu

Address: Michigan Union
330 E Liberty Street, 1st floor
Ann Arbor, MI 48109

Other Mental Health Resources:

  • Wellness Coaching - Work with a wellness coach to develop strategies for managing stress, time, and overall well-being.
  • Mindfulness - Participate in mindfulness and meditation sessions to cultivate a calm and focused mind.
  • Support Groups - Join peer support groups for shared experiences and collective coping strategies.

Community and Belonging

Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Initiatives

At the University of Michigan, we are committed to promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) across our community. Our goal is to create an environment where every member of our community feels valued and heard. If you have any questions or need assistance in finding a particular resource, please contact diversitymatters@umich.edu.

Find a comprehensive list of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion initiatives, services, and resources here.

Student Clubs and Organizations

The Center for Campus Involvement is the central hub for our 1,700 student organizations here at the University of Michigan. From your first steps looking to get involved in a group through being President of an organization, the CCI can help support all of your involvement needs.
